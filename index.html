Our Story

A recipe book, three generations deep.

Harsha grew up in a Hyderabad kitchen where spice was measured by hand and biryani was sealed shut with dough, never a lid. She learned patience before she learned knife skills — how a curry needs its full hour, how a tandoor forgives nothing rushed.

Harsha's Kitchen opened in 2012 to put that patience on a plate: recipes carried across three generations, refined with modern technique but never simplified. We grind our spice blends every morning and change the tasting menu with the seasons, but the method underneath stays exactly as she was taught it.

"A good curry can't be hurried. Every dish on this menu still takes as long as my grandmother said it should." — Harsha, Chef & Founder
